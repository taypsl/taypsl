From: Women’s March Oakland, an independently operated and funded nonprofit project of Social Good Fund

The Anti Police-Terror Project has been leading efforts to defund the police and invest in our communities for the past 5 years. Join them at today’s Oakland City Council meeting on Zoom to demand defunding of OPD's general fund budget by 50% ($150M). Can’t call in? Send an email to Oakland’s Mayor and City Council!

The Black Organizing Project has been campaigning to remove police from Oakland schools for 9 years. Sign their petition now as part of 10 days of action to pass the George Floyd Resolution to Eliminate the Oakland School Police Department on June 24.

Over the past 20 years, Urban University has delivered workforce development training and coaching to prepare more than 1,600 low-income individuals for successful entry into the workforce. Volunteer to distribute and deliver food to single mothers through the end of July as part of their Single Mamas Meal Initiative.

The East Bay LGBTQ+ Healing Arts Center offers Oakland LGBTQ+ artists of all ethnicities and areas of creative genius a safe place to heal, build and support their art throughout the year. Drop off non-perishable food donations on Wednesdays for their QommUNITY Eats Program.

Black Women Organized for Political Action (BWOPA) activates, motivates, promotes, supports and educates African-American women about the political process, encourages involvement, and affirms their commitment to, and solving of, those problems affecting the African-American community. Join BWOPA's Oakland/Berkeley Chapter virtually on June 17 to hear local leaders discuss these topics: "Where's the equity for the Black community?" and "What we need to know about the upcoming voting cycle".
